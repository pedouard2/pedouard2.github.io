Our story

Overflowed with information, it's became very hard to find new talended artists. Of course there is Pandora and the like but with such a quantity of music out there now, it's easy to end up with a background music consumed like disposable tissues.

Being truly passionate about music, we wanted to find a way to help enthusiasts discover new music in an easy and consistent way.
That's why we created the MailTape. Every sunday morning, we send you an eclectic selection of heart-touching songs curated in collaboration with the most inspiring artists. You may not like everything you hear, but if you make at least one discovery then our job is done.

Created in 2011, more than 100 artists have been featured on MailTape, such as Nicolas Jaar, Connan Mockasin, Gonzalez, Wax Tailor, Shigeto. There are many more to come.
We hope you'll enjoy this experience. Now sit back, relax and listen...

SUN MORNING, 19 JAN 2014 - EPISODE 124

  • Wendy Rene - The Same Guy

  • Donnie & Joe Emerson - Baby

  • Pender Street Steppers - Opening up song (live at Mood Hut PPU)

  • Seams - Rilo

  • Blood Cultures - Mercury Child

  • Hubert Viel - L'Amitié

  • Astrid Engberg - Ouaga Noma

This morning, discover Seams, a British whose vibe got rough after a few years in Berlin. This episode will wake you up very gently then hypnotise you like crazy under the shower to finally make you land smoothly in a warm african atmosphere.

From London to Berlin.

Seams, aka James Welch, is starting to get more and more visibility as he’s been supporting Mount Kimbie, Gold Panda and the like on tour these last 2 years.

I vividly remember this afternoon in Wellington, taking picture of the city from the rooftop of a building, when Seams’ music hit me. I was listening to his debut EP “Tourist”. The track “Nachtmusik” triggered instantly something in my brain, I then got used to play it a lot during photo shooting in streets to help me get in an almost meditative state. Since then, his music has been in constant evolution sliding progressively into something more Berlinish. Something rough, something focused on textures and rhythms. It’s been years I thought about featuring him here, now is the time, hope you enjoy.

3 tracks he likes on sunday morning

Wendy Rene - The Same Guy

"Most Sunday mornings consist of my girlfriend and I putting on a pot of coffee, some Motown, and having a leisurely breakfast. This is a track she found on SoundCloud, which has become a regular when chilling at home."

Donnie & Joe Emerson - Baby

"Again, I’m cheating and this is something discovered by my girlfriend. Although I think I may have ruined it for her by showing her the opening scene of The Comedy, which uses it to bizarre effect."

Pender Street Steppers - Opening up song (live at Mood Hut PPU)

"I can’t stop listening to these guys from Vancouver. Once the chill morning is out of the way, I stick on their tape, and get moving..."

That's all folks! Dance, love and have a great sunday. Cheers.
